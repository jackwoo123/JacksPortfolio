Mario in Unreal


Background to Project


At Ohio State, I am apart of a honors group call "Scholars" as apart of scholars, I needed to join a project group, which ranged from web development, to linkedin networking, and everything inbetween. My scholars group in particular is called MMC which stands for Media, Marketing, and Communications.



I chose the unreal project group and orginally tried to create a copy of XCOM called YCOM. I quickly realized that making a 3d game is than I anticipated. After some consideration I moved to creating level 1-1 from Super Mario World from the NES.

Coding Process


Since this class was focused on learning Unreal, we used blueprints which were very similar to actual coding. Switching from making a 3D world to a 2D pixel also reduced the difficulty tremenously, which really made the project progress smoother.



I quickly learned the importance of keeping my blueprints clean as they quickly turned into spaghetti blueprints.



Difficulties encountered

While the blueprint style of coding was easy enough to understand, I had some problems learning the "vocab" of the blueprints. For example, if statements are called branches which threw me for a "loop"


Another challenge was the camera of super mario world. The camera can only move forward and never backward. The solution that I created was using the stock platforms, I placed one just beyond the cameras view behind mario and one just ahead of the cameras view, this forward block would have no collision but would be a scanner to activate the monster's movement.


The final implementation had the pillars spawn on mario in the editor mode. Which lead to an interesting view within the editor mode of Unreal.



Final Product


When I finished baking the game, one thing I did not understand was how fast the goombas moved towards mario. They moved at 60 frames per second but were a lot slower in the editor mode when I was testing the game. I believe that because the editor mode takes more system resources, the goombas would move slower but when the game was baked, there was less system demands, and thus the goombas moved a whole lot faster.
